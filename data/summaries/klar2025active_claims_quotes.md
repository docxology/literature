## Key Claims and Hypotheses

1.  **Central Claim:** The paper proposes a novel active inference model for mouse pointing and clicking behavior, demonstrating that it can generate plausible pointing trajectories and click dynamics.
2.  **Hypothesis:**  A continuous active inference model, incorporating perceptual delay compensation and preference distributions over observations, can effectively model human pointing behavior.
3.  **Key Finding:** The AIF agent’s behavior closely resembles that of a human user, particularly in terms of end-point variance and the ability to adapt to different target difficulties.
4.  **Key Finding:** The model’s ability to generate trajectories without requiring manual parameter tuning or explicit reward functions highlights the potential of active inference for user modelling.
5.  **Key Finding:** The model’s predictive delay compensation is crucial for generating realistic trajectories, as it accounts for the inherent latency in human perception.
